## Overview

This skill provides a structured approach to conducting systematic research, analyzing information, and synthesizing findings into actionable insights. It ensures research is thorough, well-documented, and focused on answering specific questions or solving particular problems.

## When to Use This Skill

**Information Gathering**:
- Trigger: "Research X topic" or "Gather information on Y"
- Use Case: Building knowledge base for documentation or strategy

**Problem Investigation**:
- Trigger: "Investigate issue" or "Analyze problem"
- Use Case: Understanding root causes and finding solutions

**Evidence Synthesis**:
- Trigger: "Synthesize findings" or "Analyze research data"
- Use Case: Combining multiple sources into coherent insights

**Competitive Analysis**:
- Trigger: "Analyze competitors" or "Research market"
- Use Case: Understanding landscape and positioning

---

## Step 1: Define Research Objectives

**Purpose**: Clarify what questions need answering and what decisions will be informed.

**Objective Definition**:
1. **Primary Research Question**:
   - What specific question are we answering?
   - What decision will this research inform?
   - Who is the audience for this research?

2. **Secondary Questions**:
   - What sub-questions need answering?
   - What context is needed?
   - What are the key variables or factors?

3. **Success Criteria**:
   - What does "complete research" look like?
   - What evidence level is required?
   - What format should findings take?

**Validation**:
- Objectives are specific and measurable
- Success criteria are defined upfront
- Research scope is achievable

---

## Step 2: Research Planning

**Purpose**: Create systematic approach to information gathering.

**Research Strategy**:
1. **Information Sources**:
   - Primary sources (original documents, data, interviews)
   - Secondary sources (analyses, reviews, summaries)
   - Tertiary sources (textbooks, encyclopedias)
   - Domain-specific sources (academic papers, technical docs)

2. **Search Strategy**:
   - Keywords and search terms
   - Databases or platforms to search
   - Inclusion/exclusion criteria
   - Time period restrictions (if applicable)

3. **Data Collection Plan**:
   - What data points to collect
   - How to organize findings
   - Tools for tracking sources
   - Note-taking format

**Output**: Research plan with sources, search terms, and collection method

---

## Step 3: Information Gathering

**Purpose**: Collect relevant information systematically.

**Collection Methods**:
1. **Literature Review**:
   - Academic databases (Google Scholar, IEEE Xplore, etc.)
   - Industry reports and white papers
   - Technical documentation and specifications
   - Books and reference materials

2. **Web Research**:
   - Reputable websites and sources
   - Industry blogs and publications
   - Expert forums and communities
   - Vendor documentation

3. **Data Collection**:
   - Statistics and metrics
   - Case studies and examples
   - Expert opinions and quotes
   - Benchmarks and comparisons

**Quality Criteria**:
- Source credibility (author expertise, publication reputation)
- Information currency (publication date, last updated)
- Evidence quality (peer-reviewed, primary sources preferred)
- Relevance to research questions

**Output**: Organized collection of sources with notes and excerpts

---

## Step 4: Critical Analysis

**Purpose**: Evaluate information quality and extract key insights.

**Analysis Framework**:
1. **Source Evaluation**:
   - Author credibility and expertise
   - Publication bias or conflicts of interest
   - Methodology quality (for research studies)
   - Sample size and representativeness

2. **Information Synthesis**:
   - Identify patterns across sources
   - Note areas of agreement/disagreement
   - Extract key findings and data points
   - Highlight gaps in knowledge

3. **Quality Assessment**:
   - Strength of evidence (strong, moderate, weak)
   - Reliability of findings
   - Applicability to research questions
   - Limitations and caveats

**Analysis Techniques**:
- Comparative analysis (comparing sources)
- Thematic analysis (identifying themes)
- Gap analysis (finding missing information)
- Strength-of-evidence assessment

**Output**: Analyzed findings with quality ratings and key insights

---

## Step 5: Synthesis and Insights

**Purpose**: Combine findings into coherent, actionable insights.

**Synthesis Process**:
1. **Pattern Recognition**:
   - Common themes across sources
   - Recurring recommendations
   - Consistent data points
   - Emerging trends

2. **Key Findings**:
   - Top 3-5 most important insights
   - Supporting evidence for each finding
   - Confidence level in each finding
   - Implications and applications

3. **Knowledge Gaps**:
   - Questions that remain unanswered
   - Areas needing further research
   - Limitations of current knowledge
   - Recommendations for additional investigation

4. **Actionable Insights**:
   - What decisions can be made now?
   - What additional information is needed?
   - What are the recommended next steps?
   - What are the risk factors or considerations?

**Output**: Synthesis report with findings, insights, and recommendations

---

## Step 6: Documentation

**Purpose**: Create clear, usable documentation of research process and findings.

**Documentation Structure**:
1. **Research Summary**:
   - Research objectives and questions
   - Methodology and sources used
   - Key findings (executive summary)
   - Main conclusions and recommendations

2. **Detailed Findings**:
   - Organized by theme or question
   - Supporting evidence for each finding
   - Source references and citations
   - Data visualizations (charts, graphs if applicable)

3. **Appendices**:
   - Complete source list with URLs
   - Data tables and raw information
   - Methodology details
   - Quality assessment notes

4. **Recommendations**:
   - Action items based on findings
   - Areas requiring further investigation
   - Confidence levels for recommendations
   - Timeline considerations

**Documentation Best Practices**:
- Clear section headers and organization
- In-text citations for all claims
- Bibliography or reference list
- Date of research and sources
- Clear indication of evidence strength

**Output**: Complete research report with findings, sources, and recommendations

## Best Practices

**Research Quality**:
✅ **Do**:
- Start with clear research questions
- Use multiple, credible sources
- Evaluate source quality and bias
- Document methodology and sources
- Distinguish between strong and weak evidence

❌ **Don't**:
- Accept information at face value
- Rely on single sources
- Ignore source credibility
- Skip documentation methodology
- Treat all evidence as equal

**Analysis Quality**:
✅ **Do**:
- Look for patterns across sources
- Identify areas of agreement/disagreement
- Assess evidence strength critically
- Acknowledge limitations and gaps
- Provide confidence levels

❌ **Don't**:
- Cherry-pick supporting evidence
- Ignore contradictory information
- Overstate confidence in findings
- Draw conclusions beyond evidence
- Make claims without support

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Research Anti-Patterns**:
1. **Confirmation Bias**: Only seeking information that supports pre-existing beliefs
2. **Source Neglect**: Failing to evaluate source credibility or currency
3. **Scope Creep**: Expanding research beyond original objectives
4. **Analysis Paralysis**: Getting stuck in endless research without synthesis
5. **Documentation Debt**: Failing to document sources and methodology

**Analysis Anti-Patterns**:
1. **False Equivalence**: Treating weak evidence equal to strong evidence
2. **Overgeneralization**: Drawing broad conclusions from limited data
3. **Citation Neglect**: Failing to properly attribute sources
4. **Methodology Omission**: Not documenting how research was conducted
5. **Gap Ignorance**: Failing to acknowledge limitations in knowledge

## Research Tools and Techniques

**Source Evaluation**:
- CRAAP test (Currency, Relevance, Authority, Accuracy, Purpose)
- SIFT method (Stop, Investigate, Find, Trace)
- Evidence hierarchy (primary > secondary > tertiary)

**Synthesis Techniques**:
- Thematic analysis
- Comparative analysis
- Meta-analysis (for quantitative research)
- Narrative synthesis (for qualitative research)

**Documentation Tools**:
- Citation managers (Zotero, Mendeley)
- Note-taking apps (Notion, Obsidian)
- Reference management tools
- Bibliography generators
