
# Mechanical Integrity (MI) Inspection Workflow

## Purpose
To outline the Mechanical Integrity (MI) process for preventive maintenance, inspection, and testing of process equipment to comply with OSHA-KR Article 44.

## Scope
Applies to pressure vessels, storage tanks, piping systems, relief and vent systems, emergency shutdown systems, and controls.

## Process Steps

1. **Equipment Identification & Categorization**
   - Identify all equipment critical to process safety.
   - Categorize equipment based on risk and regulatory requirements.

2. **Inspection & Testing Planning**
   - Develop written procedures for inspections and tests.
   - Schedule preventive maintenance and inspections based on manufacturer recommendations, good engineering practices, and prior operating experience.

3. **Execution of Inspections**
   - Conduct inspections using trained personnel.
   - Document quantitative measurements (e.g., thickness, vibration, pressure drops).
   - Evaluate pass/fail status based on acceptance criteria.

4. **Deficiency Management**
   - If equipment fails inspection, initiate corrective actions immediately or safely shut down.
   - Document repair or replacement history.

5. **Recordkeeping**
   - Log inspection dates, names of inspectors, identifiers of equipment, descriptions of inspections, and results.
