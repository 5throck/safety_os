
# PSM Worker Training (Element 4)

## Overview
This workflow defines the procedure for conducting PSM Worker Training in compliance with OSHA-KR Article 44. All workers involved in operating or maintaining a covered process must receive initial training and periodic refresher training on the specific safety and health hazards, emergency operations, and safe work practices applicable to their job tasks.

## Scope
All workers — including contractors — who work in or near a covered process are subject to PSM training requirements. Training must address the PSM elements relevant to the worker's role and the specific hazards of the covered process.

## Procedure

### 1. Initial Training
- Provide initial training to all new workers assigned to a covered process before they begin work.
- Training must cover process-specific hazards, emergency procedures, safe work practices, and applicable PSM elements.
- Verify worker comprehension through a competency assessment or written test.

### 2. Training Content by PSM Element
- Workers must be trained on PSM elements relevant to their duties, which may include: Process Safety Information, PHA findings, Operating Procedures, Emergency Action Plan, and Mechanical Integrity.
- Document each PSM element covered during the training session in the evidence record.

### 3. Refresher Training
- Conduct refresher training at least every three years, or more frequently if:
  - Process changes occur under the Management of Change program.
  - An incident investigation reveals training deficiencies.
  - A worker's competency assessment indicates a need for retraining.
- Record the retraining due date for each worker.

### 4. Competency Verification
- Administer a competency test after each training session.
- Record the test score in the evidence model.
- Workers who do not achieve the minimum passing score must undergo retraining before returning to covered-process work.

### 5. Documentation
- Obtain the worker's acknowledgment signature confirming participation and understanding.
- Retain all training records as evidence for PSM compliance audits.
