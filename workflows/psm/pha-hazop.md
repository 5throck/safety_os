# Process Hazard Analysis (PHA) - HAZOP Methodology

## Overview
This workflow defines the procedure for conducting a Process Hazard Analysis (PHA) using the Hazard and Operability (HAZOP) methodology, in compliance with OSHA-KR Article 44.

## Procedure

### 1. Preparation
- Assemble a multi-disciplinary HAZOP team.
- Gather up-to-date Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams (P&IDs), Process Flow Diagrams (PFDs), and operating procedures.

### 2. Node Selection
- Divide the process into distinct sections or "nodes" on the P&IDs for structured analysis.
- Define the design intent and operating parameters for each node.

### 3. Deviation Analysis
- Apply guide words (e.g., MORE, LESS, NO, REVERSE, OTHER THAN) to process parameters (e.g., Flow, Pressure, Temperature, Level) to generate deviations.
- Analyze each deviation to determine potential causes.

### 4. Consequence Assessment
- Identify the potential consequences of each deviation, assuming no safeguards are active.
- Assess the severity of the consequences.

### 5. Safeguard Evaluation and Risk Grading
- Identify existing safeguards that prevent the cause or mitigate the consequence.
- Assess the risk grade based on likelihood and severity, considering the effectiveness of safeguards.

### 6. Recommendations
- If the risk grade is unacceptable, propose improvement recommendations or additional safeguards.
- Assign responsibility and deadlines for implementing recommendations.
