# Management of Change (MOC) Process Workflow

## 1. Objective
To ensure that all changes to process chemicals, technology, equipment, and facilities that affect a covered process are properly reviewed and managed before implementation, adhering to OSHA-KR Article 44.

## 2. Applicability
This workflow applies to all proposed changes in the chemical processing plant, except for "replacements in kind" (RIK).

## 3. Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Change Request Initiation
- The initiator identifies a needed change and submits an MOC request.
- Provide detailed description of the change, purpose, and technical basis.

### Step 2: Hazard Review & Risk Assessment
- Conduct a hazard and operability study (HAZOP) or relevant risk assessment for the proposed change.
- Identify potential impacts on safety, health, and the environment.

### Step 3: Technical & Safety Review
- Engineering and safety teams review the proposed change.
- Ensure compliance with applicable design codes and standards.

### Step 4: Approval
- Obtain necessary authorizations from process owners, engineering management, and safety managers before implementation.

### Step 5: Implementation & Pre-Startup Safety Review (PSSR)
- Implement the approved change.
- Conduct a PSSR before introducing highly hazardous chemicals to the modified process.

### Step 6: Training and Documentation Updates
- Train all affected employees and contractors on the change.
- Update Process Safety Information (PSI), operating procedures, and other relevant documents.

### Step 7: Closure
- Verify all requirements have been met.
- Formally close the MOC record.
